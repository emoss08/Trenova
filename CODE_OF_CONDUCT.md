# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

Trenova is built for people working through complex transportation operations. We want the repository, issue tracker, discussions, and related community spaces to be useful, respectful, and focused.

Everyone participating in this project is expected to help create an environment that is welcoming, professional, and free from harassment.

## Expected Behavior

Examples of behavior that supports a healthy community include:

- Using clear, respectful language when reporting problems or proposing changes.
- Assuming good intent while still being precise about technical concerns.
- Accepting constructive feedback and keeping disagreements focused on the work.
- Respecting different levels of experience, backgrounds, roles, and perspectives.
- Reporting bugs, regressions, security concerns, and documentation gaps responsibly.
- Avoiding speculation about people or organizations when discussing operational or business workflows.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of behavior that is not acceptable include:

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, stalking, or sustained disruption.
- Insults, personal attacks, trolling, or intentionally inflammatory comments.
- Sexualized language or imagery, unwanted sexual attention, or inappropriate advances.
- Discriminatory comments or conduct based on identity, background, ability, religion, nationality, age, or any other protected characteristic.
- Publishing private information, credentials, customer data, or internal operational details without explicit permission.
- Using the issue tracker, pull requests, discussions, or community spaces for spam, scams, unrelated promotion, or abuse.

## Reporting

If you experience or observe unacceptable behavior, report it privately by email to `eric.moss08@gmail.com`.

Please include the relevant links, screenshots, timestamps, usernames, and a short description of what happened. Do not include private customer data, credentials, or sensitive operational information unless it is strictly necessary.

Security vulnerabilities should be reported through the process in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md), not through public issues or discussions.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may take any action they determine is appropriate and proportionate, including:

- Asking a participant to clarify or edit a comment.
- Removing comments, issues, pull requests, or discussion posts.
- Temporarily or permanently limiting participation in project spaces.
- Reporting abuse to the hosting platform or other relevant service providers.

Maintainers will make reasonable efforts to evaluate reports fairly and privately. Participants who are asked to stop unacceptable behavior are expected to comply immediately.
